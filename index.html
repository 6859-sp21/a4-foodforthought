Food for Thought

By Raunak, Anushree, and Yuebin

(Note: If the visualisation doesn't fit your screen, try zooming out with ctrl + - / Cmd + -.)

We often think about our transportation, electricity, and energy use as the main contributors to global greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. However, food production and consumption make up nearly a third of global emissions and also contribute significantly to land and water use. Explore the treemap to understand the breakdown of GHG emissions across categories. Click a box to zoom in and click the top bar to zoom out one level.

In addition to drinking beverages, your diet is consuming freshwater through food production. How much water do these products use? You can find the answer by selecting a food commodity on the right and see the freshwater withdrawal in terms of nutritional units.



But how can you understand the environmental impacts of your own diet? In addition to GHG emissions and water use, land use is another often overlooked factor that causes significant environmental degradation. Select your most commonly eaten proteins on the right to find out where the distribution of land use from your diet lies.

We often think about our transportation, electricity, and energy use as the main contributors to global greenhouse gas emissions. However, food production and consumption make up nearly a third of global emissions and also contribute significantly to land and water use.

In addition to drinking beverages, your diet is consuming freshwater through food production. How much water do these products use? You can find the answer by selecting your proteins and see the freshwater withdrawal in terms of nutritional units.



But how can you understand the environmental impacts of your own diet? Select your most commonly eaten proteins on the right to find out where the distribution of GHG emissions from your diet lies.
